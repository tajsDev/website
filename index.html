Game Designer and Gamplay Engineer



Overview:
Between September and December 2023, I played a pivotal role in the development of an exceptional third-person top-down shooter for a game design class. All of the features listed are designed and implemented by me.
Primary Responsibilities as Gameplay Engineer/Designer:

Game Features:

Escalating Difficulty:
• Devised a straightforward system that gradually increased the number of enemies allowed in each level every 3 seconds, persisting across different levels.
• The mechanism discourages prolonged stays in a level, incentivizing players to complete them swiftly.

Distinctive Weapons:
• Implemented an ammunition system for the pistol, shotgun, and rifle, fine-tuning their values to maintain balance.
•This design encourages players to regularly switch weapons, ensuring a continuous stream of damage.

Adversaries and Bosses:
• Designed and programmed all enemies and bosses, determining their attack patterns, pathing, and responses to player weapons.
• Introduced unique enemies in each level to enhance difficulty and provide a distinctive feel to each stage.

Permadeath Mechanism:
• Established a permanent health system for the player that carries over between levels.
• System in place for creating a sense of tension as players face the risk of sustained damage from enemies.

Gamify Walking App ( In progress)

Flame: A Flutter Game Engine

Game Designer and Software Architect


The project I'm currently involved in is dedicated to transforming the act of walking into an enjoyable and engaging experience for children who have cerebral palsy. This initiative will leverage the innovative exoskeleton technology developed by Biomotum to serve as the primary input method for a mobile game.

At the present stage of development, my focus is primarily on crafting the structure and framework of both the mobile application and the game itself. Extensive research is being conducted to determine the most suitable game type for this project, a crucial decision that will influence the game's performance and accessibility. The ultimate aim of this undertaking is to create a mobile game that seamlessly integrates with the Biomotum exoskeleton, providing an interactive and entertaining walking experience for children with cerebral palsy.

The project is progressing towards a technical demo that is expected to be ready for presentation soon. This demo will offer a glimpse into the technical aspects of the game and how it interacts with the Biomotum exoskeleton, setting the stage for a more inclusive and enjoyable walking experience for children facing mobility challenges.
